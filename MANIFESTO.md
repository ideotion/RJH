# RJH — Manifesto

Reverse Job Hunting. Software for people who are tired of shouting into the void, built so the work finds them, on terms they can stand behind.

1. The search runs both ways. Companies should look for people as much as people look for companies. Tools that only deepen the asymmetry — spray-and-pray application bots — make the problem worse. RJH exists to rebalance the effort, not to escalate the spam.

2. A human always decides. Automation handles the drudgery; a person owns the judgement. RJH never submits an application. The final click is yours, every single time, by design and not by configuration.

3. Honesty over volume. Documents are tailored from your real experience and nothing else. The model is instructed never to fabricate. One true, well-matched application beats a hundred hollow ones.

4. Local by default. Your resume, your history, your search — they stay on your machine. Generation runs on a local model. There is no account, no upload, no telemetry.

5. Good citizen of the web. Respect robots.txt. Rate-limit. Prefer official feeds and public pages. Never defeat anti-bot measures. Being effective is not a license to be extractive.

6. Open and auditable. Open source, MIT-licensed, with a full audit trail of every action the tool takes, so anyone can see exactly what it did and why.
